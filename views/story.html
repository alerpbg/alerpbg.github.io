Our Camp: What we teach. How we teach it.

Tiny Classes

Small classes are the best way to learn, so we guarantee that our camp will have a 7:1 student-teacher ratio. Students will get the one-on-one help they need to progress at their own pace.

Scratch

Scratch is a powerful programming language developed at MIT Media Lab. With Scratch, students learn the fundamentals of programming and problem solving by snapping together colorful blocks of code.

Group Projects

Project based learning gives students a sense of accomplishment at the end of each day. Students learn to break down large projects and work with their friends to build cool games and stories.

Beginner

Our beginner class is an incredibly fun way for students to be creative and leaves campers excited about computer programming. During the course students master the fundamental aspects of programming: conditionals, loops, events, boolean, and variables. Students incorporate the new concepts they learn by building increasingly complex games, animations, and interactive stories such as Pacman and Pong. Towards the end of class students build a capstone project like Super Smash Brothers or Tron.


“I learned that there are many different ways to build anything and I learned how to choose the right way.” ~Bhuvan Belur, advanced student (July 2013).

Advanced

Our advanced class prepares students for the transition to professional programming languages by introducing them to higher-level programming concepts. Students learn about lists, iterative loops, subroutines, logic, and recursion while building sophisticated games like unbeatable Tic-Tac-Toe. Considerable emphasis is placed on using programming best practices in order to create reusable and expandable code.

FAQ

What time can I drop off and pick up my kids?
Drop off starts 30 minutes before camp starts and campers should be picked up no later than 30 minutes after camp ends.
How many students will be in my child's class?
There will be no more than 20 campers per session.
Will lunch be provided?
Lunch will not be provided, but we will have plenty of healthy snacks.
Will computers be provided or will my child need to bring their own laptop?
Students at Cedar Lane Church will be required to bring their own laptops, but Christ Episcopal School has provided us access to their computer lab. For those at Cedar Lane, loaner laptops are available for students who demonstrate need. We can also advise you on how to rent a laptop. Laptops can be either Mac or PC.
Are there scholarships available?
Definitely. Call and ask if you are interested in financial asistance or require a loaner laptop.
Who should enroll in Potomac Code Camp?
We accept children ages 10-16, but exceptions can be made. Our beginner session is for campers with no prior programming experience, and our advanced session is for campers who have completed our beginner session or have some experience with programming.
Does this class help students prepare for other programming courses such as a high school Python/Java/C++ class?
Absolutely! The beginner-level camp teaches conditionals, loops, events, and variables. The advanced class covers lists, iterative loops, code structure, logic, and recursion. All are very important concepts in any object-oriented programming language.
Will campers who attended the beginner session be able to return for the advanced session?
Of course! Our program was designed with progression in mind. Campers who attend the beginner session will be well prepared to tackle the challenges of the advanced session.
